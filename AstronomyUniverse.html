Stars are giant balls of burning gas, mostly hydrogen and helium, that produce light and heat. They form galaxies, which are massive collections of stars, dust, and dark matter bound together by gravity. Our galaxy, the Milky Way, is just one among billions.

Black holes are regions of space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They are formed when massive stars collapse. Scientists also study dark matter and dark energy, which together make up most of the universe, but remain mysterious.
